## BPDCN Overview

**Definition:** Rare, aggressive hematologic malignancy derived from plasmacytoid dendritic cells

**Key features:**
- CD123 expression (IL-3 receptor alpha) - therapeutic target
- Often presents with skin lesions
- Can involve bone marrow, lymph nodes, CNS
- Historically poor prognosis, improved with tagraxofusp

### Targeted Therapy
| Drug | Target | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Tagraxofusp** | CD123 (IL-3Rα) | First FDA-approved for BPDCN (2018) |

### Other Treatments
| Treatment | Context |
|-----------|---------|
| **AML-like induction** | Historically used (7+3, HyperCVAD) |
| **ALL-like regimens** | Alternative approach |
| **Allo-HSCT** | Consolidation in eligible patients |
| **Venetoclax-based** | Emerging option |

### Tagraxofusp Key Points
- Diphtheria toxin fused to IL-3
- **Capillary leak syndrome (CLS)** - major toxicity, requires albumin monitoring
- Administer only where CLS can be managed
- Pre-dose albumin must be ≥3.2 g/dL
