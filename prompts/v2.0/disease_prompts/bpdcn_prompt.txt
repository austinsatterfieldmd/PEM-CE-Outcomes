## BPDCN Overview

**Definition:** Rare, aggressive hematologic malignancy derived from plasmacytoid dendritic cells

**Key features:**
- CD123 expression (IL-3 receptor alpha) - primary therapeutic target
- CD4+ CD56+ CD303+ immunophenotype
- Often presents with skin lesions (purpuric nodules)
- Can involve bone marrow, lymph nodes, CNS
- Historically poor prognosis, improved with tagraxofusp

**No staging system** - BPDCN doesn't use conventional staging

### Targeted Therapy
| Drug | Target | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Tagraxofusp** | CD123 (IL-3Rα) | First FDA-approved for BPDCN (2018), diphtheria toxin conjugate |

### Other Treatments
| Treatment | Context |
|-----------|---------|
| **AML-like induction** | Historically used (7+3, HyperCVAD) |
| **ALL-like regimens** | Alternative approach |
| **Allo-HSCT** | Consolidation in eligible patients, may be curative |
| **Venetoclax-based** | Emerging option, often with HMA |

### Tagraxofusp Key Points
- Diphtheria toxin fused to IL-3 (recombinant fusion protein)
- **Capillary leak syndrome (CLS)** - major dose-limiting toxicity
- Administer only where CLS can be managed
- **Pre-dose albumin must be ≥3.2 g/dL** - critical threshold
- Hepatotoxicity (monitor LFTs)

### ⚠️ Topic Disambiguation

**"Indication" vs "Efficacy":**
- "Is tagraxofusp **indicated** for BPDCN?" → **Treatment indication**
- "What is the response rate with tagraxofusp?" → **Clinical efficacy**

**"Safety profile" vs "Prophylaxis" vs "AE management":**
- **Safety profile** = knowing WHAT AEs to expect (awareness)
- **Prophylaxis** = how to PREVENT toxicities BEFORE they occur (albumin monitoring before each dose)
- **AE management** = what to DO after an AE has occurred (managing active CLS)
