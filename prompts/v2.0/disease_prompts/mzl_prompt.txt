## MZL Overview

**MZL subtypes:**
- **Extranodal MZL (MALT lymphoma)** - Most common, associated with chronic infection/inflammation
- **Splenic MZL** - Splenomegaly, often HCV-associated
- **Nodal MZL** - Least common, behaves more aggressively

**Key features:**
- Indolent B-cell lymphoma
- Often localized at diagnosis
- Some cases linked to infectious triggers (H. pylori gastric MALT, HCV splenic MZL)

## MZL Drug Landscape

### Frontline Treatment
| Setting | Treatment |
|---------|-----------|
| Localized MALT | Radiation, antibiotics (if H. pylori+) |
| Advanced/Symptomatic | Rituximab ± chemotherapy |

### Systemic Options
| Drug/Regimen | Context |
|--------------|---------|
| **Rituximab** | Single-agent or combination |
| **Bendamustine + Rituximab (BR)** | Common combination |
| **Lenalidomide + Rituximab (R2)** | Alternative option |
| **Zanubrutinib** | FDA approved for MZL (MAGNOLIA) |
| **Ibrutinib** | BTK inhibitor option |

### Special Situations
- **H. pylori+ gastric MALT:** Antibiotic eradication first
- **HCV+ splenic MZL:** Antiviral therapy may induce remission
