## ⚠️ CRITICAL: Non-Oncology Question Detection (SID Questions)

**BEFORE tagging, verify this is truly an ONCOLOGY question.** Questions that MENTION a hematologic malignancy may actually be about a different specialty entirely.

### Secondary Immunodeficiency (SID) Questions — Flag as NON-ONCOLOGY

Patients with MM, CLL, and other hematologic malignancies often develop secondary immunodeficiency. Questions about **managing infections or diagnosing immunodeficiency** in these patients belong to **Infectious Disease/Immunology**, NOT oncology.

**Red flags that suggest NON-ONCOLOGY (SID):**
1. Activity name contains "SID", "Secondary Immunodeficiency", or "Immunodeficiency"
2. Patient has a hematologic malignancy BUT the question asks about:
   - Infection diagnosis/management (CMV, PJP, fungal, etc.)
   - Immunoglobulin replacement therapy
   - Vaccination in immunocompromised patients
   - Tests to diagnose immunodeficiency (not cancer)
3. Answer choices focus on immunology/infectious disease concepts, NOT cancer treatment

**Example of a NON-ONCOLOGY question that LOOKS like MM:**
> "A patient with multiple myeloma actively undergoing treatment with chemotherapy presents with acute cytomegalovirus infection. At baseline, what hematological tests should be performed to confirm or rule out secondary immunodeficiency?"
>
> **Why this is NON-ONCOLOGY:** The question is about diagnosing secondary immunodeficiency, not treating MM. The MM is background context. Answer choices are immunology tests (immunoglobulin levels, antibody production), not oncology treatments.

**If you detect this pattern:**
- Tag `topic: "Non-oncology - SID"` to flag for manual review
- The question will be removed from the oncology dataset and reclassified

**True oncology questions in infection context:**
- "How should you modify bortezomib dosing during active infection?" → ONCOLOGY (about MM treatment modification)
- "Which MM treatment has highest infection risk?" → ONCOLOGY (about MM drug safety)

## CRITICAL: Compound Answer Handling

When the correct answer references options by letter/number (e.g., "B or C", "A and C", "Options 1 and 3"):

1. **DO NOT assume option order matches the original question** - The options you receive may be in a different order than the actual test question.

2. **Deduce the correct answer from clinical reasoning** - Read the substance of each option and determine which ones make sense as the correct answer based on the clinical scenario.

3. **Tag based on the deduced correct content** - Once you identify what the compound answer actually refers to, tag those treatments/concepts.

**Example (CLL):**
- **Stem:** 71-year-old man with treatment-naive CLL, unmutated IGHV, del(13q), no TP53 abnormality. On DOAC for atrial fibrillation, prefers minimal cardiac toxicity. What is the most appropriate frontline treatment?
- **Correct answer:** "B or C"
- **Options presented to you:** Acalabrutinib plus venetoclax ± obinutuzumab | Continuous ibrutinib monotherapy | Venetoclax plus obinutuzumab

**How to deduce:** The patient wants to avoid cardiac toxicity and has AFib. Ibrutinib has higher AFib risk than other BTKis. Therefore "B or C" most likely refers to:
- Acalabrutinib-based regimen (2nd-gen BTKi, lower cardiac risk)
- Venetoclax-obinutuzumab (no BTKi cardiac risk)

**Tag accordingly:** treatment_1: Acalabrutinib, treatment_2: Venetoclax, treatment_3: Obinutuzumab

## Group A: Core Fields (20 fields)

### 1. topic
**REQUIRED** - Every question must have exactly one topic. Never null.

| Topic | Use When... |
|-------|-------------|
| **Treatment selection** | Choosing WHICH drug/regimen to use for a patient (patient vignette + "which therapy") |
| **Treatment indication** | Whether a patient MEETS CRITERIA for a treatment — eligibility, candidacy, **approved indications**, contraindications. "Which eligibility criteria for CAR-T?", "Does age exclude transplant?", "Where is X indicated?" |
| **Clinical efficacy** | Response rates, survival outcomes, clinical trial results, "key findings", **"where has X demonstrated efficacy"** |

**⚠️ Topic Disambiguation - "Indication" vs "Efficacy":**
- "Where is venetoclax **indicated**?" → **Treatment indication** (asking about approved label/indications)
- "Where has venetoclax **demonstrated efficacy**?" → **Clinical efficacy** (asking about trial results/outcomes)
- Key distinction: "indicated" = regulatory approval; "demonstrated efficacy" = clinical evidence

**⚠️ Topic Disambiguation - Safety profile vs Prophylaxis vs AE management (CRITICAL THREE-WAY):**
- **Safety profile** = knowing WHAT adverse events to expect (awareness, education, recognition)
- **Prophylaxis** = knowing HOW TO PREVENT toxicities BEFORE they occur (prevention, premedication, mitigation)
- **AE management** = knowing WHAT TO DO after an AE has already occurred (intervention, dose modification)

Key test: What is the educational focus?
- "What AEs should patients expect on bispecifics?" → **Safety profile** (awareness)
- "What prophylaxis is needed before starting bispecifics?" → **Prophylaxis** (prevention)
- "Patient develops Grade 2 CRS on teclistamab. What do you do?" → **AE management** (intervention)

**MM-specific Prophylaxis examples:**
- "What antiviral prophylaxis is needed with bortezomib?" → **Prophylaxis**; treatment_1: "Bortezomib"
- "Which treatments require IVIg prophylaxis?" → **Prophylaxis**; treatment_1: the relevant bispecific/CAR-T
- "Patients starting daratumumab need prophylaxis for which virus?" → **Prophylaxis**; treatment_1: "Daratumumab"

**Treatment tagging for Prophylaxis questions:** Tag the drug(s) that CAUSE the toxicity requiring prophylaxis, NOT the prophylactic agent itself.
- Question about IVIg prophylaxis for bispecific-induced hypogammaglobulinemia → treatment_1: "Teclistamab", NOT "IVIg"
- Question about acyclovir prophylaxis for bortezomib-induced VZV → treatment_1: "Bortezomib", NOT "Acyclovir"

**When NOT to use Prophylaxis (even if prophylaxis is mentioned):**
- If the question is primarily about care coordination, referrals, or enrollment → **Multidisciplinary care**
- Example: "You are coordinating enrollment for bispecific therapy and must counsel on CRS/ICANS and arrange referrals." → **Multidisciplinary care** (focus is on coordination, not prophylaxis itself)

## Group C: Treatment Metadata (10 fields)

### 15. drug_class_1 through drug_class_3
Therapeutic class of the fundable drug(s).
**MM examples:** `"Proteasome inhibitor"`, `"IMiD"`, `"Monoclonal antibody"`, `"CAR-T therapy"`, `"Bispecific antibody"`, `"Antibody-drug conjugate (ADC)"`, `"Nuclear export inhibitor"`, `"CELMoD"`.

**CRITICAL — Use GENERIC drug class names.** The target (BCMA, GPRC5D, CD38, SLAMF7) goes in `drug_target_1-3`, NOT in drug_class.

**Monoclonal antibody rule:**
- ALL monoclonal antibodies use `drug_class: "Monoclonal antibody"` — distinguish by drug_target
- Daratumumab, Isatuximab: `drug_class: "Monoclonal antibody"` + `drug_target: "CD38"`
- Elotuzumab: `drug_class: "Monoclonal antibody"` + `drug_target: "SLAMF7"`
- This enables analysis of mAbs as a class while distinguishing mechanisms by target

**Examples:**
- Correct: `drug_class_1: "Monoclonal antibody"` + `drug_target_1: "CD38"` (for daratumumab, isatuximab)
- Correct: `drug_class_1: "Monoclonal antibody"` + `drug_target_1: "SLAMF7"` (for elotuzumab)
- Incorrect: `drug_class_1: "Anti-CD38 mAb"` (redundant with drug_target)
- Correct: `drug_class_1: "CAR-T therapy"` + `drug_target_1: "BCMA"`
- Incorrect: `drug_class_1: "BCMA CAR-T"`
- Correct: `drug_class_1: "Bispecific antibody"` + `drug_target_1: "GPRC5D"`
- Incorrect: `drug_class_1: "GPRC5D bispecific"`
- Correct: `drug_class_1: "Bispecific antibody"` + `drug_target_1: "BCMA"`
- Incorrect: `drug_class_1: "BCMA bispecific"`

### 16. drug_target_1 through drug_target_3
Molecular target(s) of the drug mechanism.
**MM examples:** `"Proteasome"`, `"Cereblon"`, `"CD38"`, `"BCMA"`, `"SLAMF7"`, `"XPO1"`, `"GPRC5D"`.

**CRITICAL - BCMA and GPRC5D:**
- These are drug targets (what the drug binds to), **NOT biomarkers**
- Tag as `drug_target`, NEVER as `biomarker`
- All MM cells express these targets; expression is not tested for patient selection
- **DO NOT TAG CD3** - For bispecifics (teclistamab, elranatamab, talquetamab), ONLY tag the tumor target (BCMA or GPRC5D). CD3 is the T-cell engager component and should NOT be tagged as a drug target
- **ONLY ONE drug target for bispecifics**: drug_target_1 = "BCMA" or "GPRC5D", drug_target_2 = null

### 17. prior_therapy_1 through prior_therapy_3
Relevant prior treatments if mentioned.

**CRITICAL: Prefer drug CLASS over specific drug names.**
- Use `"Prior PI"` instead of "Prior bortezomib" or "Prior carfilzomib"
- Use `"Prior IMiD"` instead of "Prior lenalidomide" or "Prior pomalidomide"
- Use `"Prior anti-CD38"` instead of "Prior daratumumab" or "Prior isatuximab"
- Use `"Prior CAR-T"` or `"Prior bispecific"` instead of specific product names

**Only use specific drug names when:**
- The drug is unique in its indication
- The question specifically discusses that drug's unique properties
- Documenting refractory status (e.g., "Lenalidomide-refractory")

**MM examples:** `"Prior PI"`, `"Prior IMiD"`, `"Prior anti-CD38"`, `"Prior ASCT"`, `"Prior CAR-T"`, `"Prior bispecific"`, `"Lenalidomide-refractory"`, `"Triple-class refractory"`.

### 18. resistance_mechanism
If resistance is discussed.
**MM examples:** `"Lenalidomide-refractory"`, `"PI-refractory"`, `"Triple-class refractory"`, `"Penta-refractory"`.

## Group H: Question Format/Quality (13 fields)

This section evaluates both the educational level AND the structural quality of the question for internal QA.

### 30. cme_outcome_level
Moore's CME outcome level.
**Valid values:**
- `"3 - Knowledge"` - Question tests recall of facts/information
- `"4 - Competence"` - Question tests ability to apply knowledge to clinical scenarios

**CRITICAL RULE:** If the clinical vignette is NOT REQUIRED to answer the question, it's **Level 3 (Knowledge)**, not Level 4.

**Test:** Could someone answer this question correctly WITHOUT reading the patient details?
- YES → `"3 - Knowledge"` (the vignette is just context/decoration)
- NO → `"4 - Competence"` (must apply knowledge to the specific patient)

### 31. data_response_type
Type of response elicited.
**Valid values:**
- `"Numeric"` - Answer involves numbers/statistics
- `"Qualitative"` - Answer involves descriptions/categories
- `"Comparative"` - Answer compares options/outcomes
- `"Boolean"` - Answer is yes/no or true/false

### 32. stem_type
Question stem format.
**Valid values:**
- `"Clinical vignette"` - Patient case scenario
- `"Direct question"` - Straightforward knowledge question
- `"Incomplete statement"` - Fill-in-the-blank style

### 33. lead_in_type
Question lead-in format.
**Valid values:**
- `"Standard"` - Regular question format
- `"Negative (EXCEPT/NOT)"` - Asks for the exception
- `"Best answer"` - "Which is the BEST/MOST..." format
- `"True statement"` - "Which statement is TRUE"

### 34. answer_format
Answer structure.
**Valid values:**
- `"Single best"` - Select one best answer
- `"Compound (A+B)"` - Options like "A and B", "B and C"
- `"All of above"` - Includes "All of the above"
- `"None of above"` - Includes "None of the above"
- `"True-False"` - True/False options

### 35. answer_length_pattern
Pattern of answer option lengths. **DEFAULT: "Uniform"**

**PURPOSE**: This field is for **question quality auditing**. We want to identify questions where answer length could be a test-taking cue (e.g., correct answer is always longest).

**CRITICAL: Most questions are "Uniform".** Use "Variable" ONLY when answer options are fundamentally different TYPES, not just slightly different word counts.

**DEFAULT: If `distractor_homogeneity` = "Homogeneous", ALWAYS tag `answer_length_pattern` = "Uniform".**

Override to "Variable" or "Correct longest/shortest" ONLY when the length difference is EGREGIOUS — e.g., one answer is a single word and another is a full sentence with additional clinical detail.

Do NOT tag "Variable" just because:
- One option has a few extra words
- Compound answers are longer ("Both A and B" vs "A only")
- Drug name lengths differ

**When in doubt with Homogeneous distractors → tag "Uniform".**

**Valid values:**
- `"Uniform"` - **DEFAULT (90%+ of questions).** All options are conceptually similar in type and scope. Minor length differences due to drug names, combo regimens, or acronym expansion do NOT make it Variable.
- `"Variable"` - **RARE.** Only when options are fundamentally different types — typically correlates with "Heterogeneous" distractors. Example: 3 options are drug names, but 1 option is a multi-sentence clinical statement.
- `"Correct longest"` - Correct answer is dramatically longer than all distractors (flaw indicator)
- `"Correct shortest"` - Correct answer is dramatically shorter than all distractors

**IMPORTANT:** If answer lengths ARE variable AND the correct answer is the longest → tag `"Correct longest"` (not just "Variable"). Similarly for shortest. These are more specific than "Variable" and should be used when applicable.

**What does NOT make a question "Variable":**
- Acronym expansion: "IVIg (intravenous immunoglobulin)" vs "Acyclovir" → Still **Uniform** (grammar rule, not content difference)
- Combo vs single drugs: "DRd (daratumumab, lenalidomide, dexamethasone)" vs "Bortezomib" → Still **Uniform** (both are treatment options)
- Slightly longer phrasing: "High-risk cytogenetics" vs "Standard-risk" → Still **Uniform** (same conceptual type)
- Compound answers: "Dara-VRd only" / "VRd only" / "Both Dara-VRd and VRd" → Still **Uniform** (third just combines options, no new clinical content)

**What DOES make a question "Variable":**
- Mixed types: "Daratumumab" / "Bortezomib" / "Lenalidomide" / "Continue current therapy and monitor disease progression with serial M-protein levels" → **Variable** (3 drug names vs 1 management statement)
- Dramatically different scopes: Short answer options vs one that's a full paragraph → **Variable**

**Key insight**: Variable length pattern is ALWAYS tied to Heterogeneous distractors. If distractors are Homogeneous (all same type), length is ALWAYS Uniform.

### 36. distractor_homogeneity
Whether answer options are structurally similar (same TYPE) or fundamentally different.

**PURPOSE**: This field is for **question quality auditing**. Heterogeneous distractors can indicate poor question design (mixing apples and oranges).

**Valid values:**
- `"Homogeneous"` - **DEFAULT for well-written questions.** All options are the **same TYPE** — all drug names, all regimens, all clinical phrases about similar concepts. The content differs, but the TYPE is consistent. If distractors are similar enough that they don't raise question quality concerns, use Homogeneous.
- `"Heterogeneous"` - Options are **fundamentally different TYPES** that mix categories. Examples: drug names mixed with dosing instructions, treatment options mixed with monitoring recommendations, short answers mixed with multi-sentence statements.

**Decision rule**: Ask "Are these all the same kind of answer?" If yes → Homogeneous. If they're mixing different categories → Heterogeneous.

**Examples:**
- **Homogeneous**: "Daratumumab" / "Isatuximab" / "Elotuzumab" / "Carfilzomib" (all drug names)
- **Homogeneous**: "VRd" / "DRd" / "KRd" / "Rd" (all regimen abbreviations)
- **Homogeneous**: "High-risk cytogenetics" / "Standard-risk" / "Del(17p)" / "t(4;14)" (all risk/cytogenetic categories)
- **Heterogeneous**: "Daratumumab" / "Increase monitoring frequency" / "Refer to transplant" / "No change in management" (mixing drug choice, monitoring, referral, watchful waiting)

### Item Writing Flaw Fields (36-41)
These 6 fields are **boolean** (true/false). Set to `true` if the flaw is present, `false` if not.

### 37. flaw_absolute_terms
`true` if answer options contain testwise cues like "always", "never", "all", "none" that make them less likely to be correct.

### 38. flaw_grammatical_cue
`true` if stem grammar reveals the answer (e.g., "an" before vowel-starting answer, singular/plural mismatch).

### 39. flaw_implausible_distractor
`true` if any distractor is obviously wrong and would not be chosen by a competent hematologist.

### 40. flaw_clang_association
`true` if the correct answer shares unusual words or phrases with the stem that could guide to the answer without knowledge.

### 41. flaw_convergence_vulnerability
`true` if multiple options share elements and the correct answer contains all shared elements (test-taking strategy vulnerability).

### 42. flaw_double_negative
`true` if there's a negative stem combined with negative answer option creating confusion.

## Important Guidelines

1. **Explicit staging for MM** - disease_stage is ONLY tagged when R-ISS or ISS is explicitly stated; default is null
2. **Transplant eligibility moved** - Use `treatment_eligibility` field, NOT disease_type
3. **Cytogenetic risk in disease_type** - High-risk/Standard-risk goes in disease_type_1
4. **Treatment line terminology** - Use "Newly diagnosed" and "R/R", NOT "1L" and "2L+"
5. **Multi-drug regimens** - MM often uses 3-4 drug combinations; tag all fundable drugs
6. **Don't tag dexamethasone** - Always part of backbone, not fundable
7. **BCMA is a drug target, NOT a biomarker** - For BCMA-directed therapies (CAR-T, bispecifics), tag `drug_target_1: "BCMA"`, leave biomarker fields null
8. **Tag content from the CORRECT answer only**: Only tag treatments, biomarkers, cytogenetics, and other entities that appear in the CORRECT answer, NOT the distractors (incorrect options). The distractors are wrong/not applicable by definition.
   - **Standard questions ("Which of the following...")**: Tag ONLY entities from the correct answer
   - **Negative lead-ins ("All EXCEPT", "Which is NOT")**: Tag the INCORRECT options instead, as they represent the true/factual statements
   - **Compound answers ("A and B", "All of the above")**: Tag all components that make up the correct answer
   - **Example**: If Q asks "Which cytogenetic abnormality is associated with sensitivity to venetoclax?" and A: t(11;14), B: t(4;14), C: del(17p), D: gain(1q) with correct answer A, ONLY tag t(11;14) as biomarker
9. **Infer from context** - Trials, drugs, and clinical context can imply eligibility, line, and risk
