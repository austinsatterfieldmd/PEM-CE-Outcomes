## disease_type: CRITICAL - Castration Status

**PROSTATE CANCER'S #1 DISTINGUISHING FEATURE IS CASTRATION STATUS.**

The disease_type field captures **castration status**, NOT molecular subtypes (unlike breast cancer or NSCLC).

### Castration Status Hierarchy

| disease_type Value | Definition | Key Indicators |
|-------------------|------------|----------------|
| `"mCRPC"` | **Metastatic Castration-Resistant Prostate Cancer** | Metastases + rising PSA despite castrate testosterone (<50 ng/dL) |
| `"nmCRPC"` | **Non-Metastatic Castration-Resistant Prostate Cancer** | No distant metastases + rising PSA despite castrate testosterone |
| `"mHSPC"` | **Metastatic Hormone-Sensitive Prostate Cancer** | Metastases + responds to ADT (castrate testosterone not yet reached or PSA still falling) |
| `"Localized high-risk"` | High-risk localized disease | Gleason ≥8, PSA >20, or cT3-T4 |
| `"Localized intermediate-risk"` | Intermediate-risk localized | Gleason 7, PSA 10-20, or cT2b-c |
| `"Localized low-risk"` | Low-risk localized | Gleason ≤6, PSA <10, cT1-T2a |
| `"Neuroendocrine"` | Neuroendocrine prostate cancer | Treatment-emergent NEPC, small cell features, low/absent PSA |
| `null` | Default if castration status unclear | |

### Critical Decision Points

**mCRPC vs mHSPC:**
- If "castration-resistant", "CRPC", "castrate testosterone", "rising PSA on ADT" → mCRPC
- If "hormone-sensitive", "HSPC", "de novo metastatic", "initiating ADT" → mHSPC
- If metastatic + on ADT but no mention of resistance → mHSPC (default to hormone-sensitive)

**nmCRPC:**
- Rising PSA + castrate testosterone + NO distant metastases (M0)
- Often mentioned in context of apalutamide (SPARTAN), enzalutamide (PROSPER), darolutamide (ARAMIS)

**Neuroendocrine:**
- Look for "small cell", "neuroendocrine", "lineage plasticity", "low PSA with high disease burden"
- Often treatment-emergent after ARSI therapy

## treatment_line: Prostate Cancer Conventions

**Localized/Locally Advanced:**
- `"Neoadjuvant"` - Before surgery or radiation
- `"Adjuvant"` - After surgery (for high-risk features)
- `"Salvage"` - For biochemical recurrence after definitive therapy

**Metastatic Hormone-Sensitive (mHSPC):**
- `"1L"` - First-line systemic therapy (ADT ± intensification)
- Treatment intensification refers to adding ARSI or docetaxel to ADT

**Castration-Resistant (CRPC):**
- `"1L"` - First treatment after developing castration resistance
- `"2L+"` - Second-line or later in CRPC setting
- Note: Count lines WITHIN CRPC, not overall (e.g., abiraterone for mHSPC → enzalutamide for mCRPC = "1L" CRPC)

**Maintenance:**
- `"Maintenance"` - Continuation therapy (rare in prostate cancer)

## Edge Cases and Special Considerations

### 1. Screening vs Staging Imaging
- **Screening:** mpMRI for detecting cancer in asymptomatic patients → NON-ONCOLOGY
- **Staging:** PSMA PET for newly diagnosed high-risk prostate cancer → ONCOLOGY (tag as "Biomarker testing")

### 2. Counting Treatment Lines in CRPC
- **Key Principle:** Count lines WITHIN the CRPC setting, NOT overall treatment lines
- Example: ADT + abiraterone (mHSPC) → progresses → enzalutamide (mCRPC) = "1L" in CRPC setting

### 3. Combination Therapies (PARP + ARSI)
- Newer trials use PARP inhibitor + ARSI combinations (TALAPRO-2, MAGNITUDE)
- Tag both drugs: treatment_1: "talazoparib", treatment_2: "enzalutamide"
- Tag both drug classes: drug_class_1: "PARP inhibitor", drug_class_2: "ARSI"

### 4. Neuroendocrine Transformation
- Look for "lineage plasticity", "small cell features", "undetectable PSA with disease progression"
- disease_type: "Neuroendocrine"
- Treatment shifts to platinum-based chemotherapy (NOT fundable, don't tag)

### 5. PSA Flare vs Progression
- PSA can transiently rise after starting ADT ("PSA flare") - not progression
- True progression: sustained PSA rise after initial response

### 6. PSMA PET vs FDG PET
- PSMA PET: detects PSMA-expressing disease (most prostate cancers)
- FDG PET: detects high-grade, aggressive disease (PSMA-negative variants)
- If "discordant PSMA non-avid disease" → consider tagging as aggressive/neuroendocrine features

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Don't confuse mHSPC with mCRPC:**
   - "De novo metastatic" → mHSPC
   - "Rising PSA on ADT" → mCRPC

2. **Don't tag ADT/LHRH agonists unless they're the focus:**
   - ADT is the backbone, not the fundable treatment

3. **Don't tag screening questions as oncology:**
   - "Who should be screened?" → NON-ONCOLOGY
   - "Should we do PSMA PET in newly diagnosed high-risk?" → ONCOLOGY (staging)

4. **Don't forget castration status in disease_type:**
   - This is THE defining feature of prostate cancer

5. **Don't tag PSA as biomarker unless it's explicitly the focus:**
   - PSA is mentioned in almost every question - only tag when PSA itself is being discussed

6. **HRR mutations ≠ BRCA only:**
   - BRCA1/2 are the most common, but ATM, PALB2, CHEK2 also qualify for PARP inhibitors

---

## Summary: Key Takeaways

1. **Castration status is EVERYTHING** - this goes in disease_type
2. **Screening questions are NON-ONCOLOGY** - flag them
3. **Count treatment lines WITHIN CRPC** - not overall
4. **PSMA-positive = radioligand eligible** - tag as biomarker
5. **HRR mutations = PARP inhibitor eligible** - tag as biomarker
6. **High-volume mHSPC = triplet therapy** (ADT + ARSI + docetaxel)
7. **Neuroendocrine transformation = platinum chemo** (not ARSI)
