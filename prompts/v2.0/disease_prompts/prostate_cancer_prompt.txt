### 2. disease_stage
**OPTIONAL** - Tag when staging information is explicitly mentioned or clearly inferable.

**Valid values:**
- `"Localized"` - Stage I-II, T1-T2, organ-confined, no metastases, curative intent
- `"Locally advanced"` - Stage III, T3-T4, extracapsular extension, seminal vesicle invasion, high-risk but may still be curative
- `"Biochemical recurrence"` - Rising PSA after definitive therapy (surgery/radiation) with NO visible metastases on imaging
- `"Metastatic"` - Stage IV, M1, bone metastases, lymph node metastases, visceral metastases, systemic therapy required

**When to tag:**
- Staging explicitly mentioned ("Stage III", "T3N0M0", "M1 disease")
- Clinical scenario clearly indicates stage (e.g., "bone metastases" → Metastatic)
- Post-treatment status ("PSA relapse after prostatectomy" → Biochemical recurrence)

**When null is appropriate:**
- General questions about drug mechanisms without stage context
- Questions applicable across multiple stages
- Stage not mentioned or inferable

**IMPORTANT - Biochemical Recurrence is Distinct:**
- BCR = rising PSA after treatment, NO visible metastases (M0)
- This is different from metastatic disease (M1)
- BCR has specific management (salvage RT, early systemic therapy, observation)

**CRITICAL - DO NOT CONFUSE:**
- Castration status (mCRPC vs mHSPC) goes in **disease_type**, NOT disease_stage
- "Metastatic" in disease_stage refers to presence of metastases
- Castration resistance refers to disease behavior, not metastatic burden

## Group G: Question Format/Quality (13 fields)

### 59. cme_outcome_level
**REQUIRED** - Every question must have a CME level. Never null.

**Valid values:**
- `"3 - Knowledge"` - Tests recall of facts, definitions, mechanisms
- `"4 - Competence"` - Tests application to clinical scenarios

**CRITICAL RULE:** If the clinical vignette is NOT REQUIRED to answer the question, it's **Level 3 (Knowledge)**, not Level 4.

**Test:** Could someone answer this question correctly WITHOUT reading the patient details?
- YES → `"3 - Knowledge"` (the vignette is just context/decoration)
- NO → `"4 - Competence"` (must apply knowledge to the specific patient)

**Examples:**
- "What is the mechanism of action of enzalutamide?" → cme_outcome_level: "3 - Knowledge" (fact recall)
- "A 65-year-old man with mCRPC and BRCA2 mutation... which treatment?" → cme_outcome_level: "4 - Competence" (application to patient)

**Valid values:**

- `"Numeric"` - Number answer (5-year survival rate, PSA value)
- `"Comparative"` - Comparing two or more options (A vs B vs C)

### 62. lead_in_type
**REQUIRED** - Every question must have a lead-in type. Never null.

**Valid values:**
- `"Standard"` - "What is...", "Which treatment..."
- `"Negative (EXCEPT/NOT)"` - "Which is NOT true?", "All EXCEPT..."
- `"Best answer"` - "What is the BEST next step?", "MOST appropriate..."
- `"True statement"` - "Which is true?"

### 63. answer_format
**REQUIRED** - Every question must have an answer format. Never null.

**Valid values:**
- `"Single best"` - One correct answer (most common)
- `"Compound (A+B)"` - Combination answer (e.g., "A and C")
- `"All of above"` - All options are correct
- `"None of above"` - None of the options are correct

**⚠️ CRITICAL: Only tag items from the QUESTION STEM and CORRECT ANSWER**

Do NOT tag treatments, biomarkers, trials, or other entities mentioned ONLY in incorrect/distractor answers.

**When to consider incorrect answers:**
- `"Compound (A+B)"` format → You may need to evaluate multiple options being compared
- `"All of above"` / `"None of above"` → Consider all options to determine correctness
- `"Single best"` → **ONLY tag items from question stem + correct answer**

**Why this matters:** Distractors test whether learners can distinguish correct from incorrect information. Tagging distractors pollutes the dataset with drugs/biomarkers that are NOT actually the focus of the question.

**Examples:**
- Q: "Which ARSI is indicated for nmCRPC?" Correct: "Apalutamide", Wrong: "Pembrolizumab"
  - Tag: `treatment_1: "Apalutamide"` (NOT pembrolizumab - it's a distractor)
- Q: "Which of the following are true? A and C" (Compound format)
  - Tag: Items from A, B, C, D as needed to capture what's being evaluated

### 66. answer_length_pattern
**REQUIRED** - Every question must have an answer length pattern. Never null.

**Valid values:**
- `"Uniform"` - All answer options approximately same length
- `"Variable"` - Mix of short and long answers
- `"Correct longest"` - Correct answer is noticeably longest
- `"Correct shortest"` - Correct answer is noticeably shortest

**⚠️ CRITICAL DEFAULT RULE:**
If `distractor_homogeneity` = "Homogeneous", you MUST tag `answer_length_pattern` = "Uniform" UNLESS the length difference is EGREGIOUS (see examples below).

**Rule of thumb:** If you're debating whether it's "Variable", it's probably "Uniform". Only use "Variable" when the difference is obvious and extreme.

**When to override to "Variable":**
Override ONLY when there is an EXTREME disparity in answer length or detail level:
- ✓ One option is a single word ("Olaparib") and another is a full clinical sentence with multiple concepts ("Enzalutamide with continuation of ADT and close monitoring for PSA progression every 3 months")
- ✓ One option is 3-4 words and another is 15+ words with multiple distinct clinical actions

**When to keep "Uniform" despite minor length differences:**
- ✗ All options are treatment regimens with similar structure, even if one has slightly more detail (e.g., "Abiraterone plus ADT" vs "Apalutamide")
- ✗ All options are clinical management choices (e.g., "Hold therapy", "Switch therapy", "Monitor PSA"), even if one is 2-3 words longer
- ✗ All options are biomarker tests with similar conceptual complexity

**"Uniform"** = All options are CONCEPTUALLY similar in complexity:
- All single drug names: "Abiraterone" / "Enzalutamide" / "Olaparib" → **Uniform**
- All short phrases: "Continue therapy" / "Switch treatment" / "Monitor PSA" → **Uniform**
- All treatment regimens: "Abiraterone plus ADT" / "Apalutamide plus ADT" / "Enzalutamide" → **Uniform** (even if word counts differ slightly)

**"Variable"** = Options differ in TYPE or COMPLEXITY with EGREGIOUS disparity:
- Mix of single words and detailed explanations: "Abiraterone" / "Continue current therapy and monitor for progression with serial PSA testing and imaging every 3 months" → **Variable**

**"Correct longest/shortest"** = The CORRECT answer is noticeably longer/shorter than ALL distractors

---

**Example - Question #602 (Uniform despite variation):**
All answer options are treatment recommendations for biochemical recurrence:
- "Observation with PSA monitoring"
- "Abiraterone plus ADT"
- "Apalutamide plus ADT"
- "Enzalutamide"
→ answer_length_pattern: **"Uniform"** (all are treatment recommendations with similar conceptual complexity)
→ distractor_homogeneity: **"Homogeneous"** (all treatment options)
**Rationale:** While word counts differ slightly (2-4 words), all options represent the SAME TYPE of response (treatment choices) with similar structure. Default to "Uniform" when distractors are homogeneous.

**Example - Question #729 (Actually Variable - egregious difference):**
- Option A: "Enzalutamide plus talazoparib" (3 words)
- Option B: "Continued enzalutamide monotherapy" (3 words)
- Option C: "Hold enzalutamide" (2 words)
- Option D: "Switch to abiraterone with close monitoring for disease progression and consideration of alternative targeted therapies based on updated molecular profiling" (20+ words with multiple clinical actions)
→ answer_length_pattern: **"Variable"** (Option D is EGREGIOUSLY longer with multiple distinct concepts)
→ distractor_homogeneity: **"Homogeneous"** (all treatment management choices, but override justified)
**Rationale:** Option D is not just longer - it contains multiple distinct clinical actions (switch + monitor + consider alternatives + molecular profiling), while A/B/C are simple 2-3 word treatment choices. This is an EGREGIOUS difference that justifies overriding the "Uniform" default.

### 67. distractor_homogeneity
**REQUIRED** - Every question must have a distractor homogeneity rating. Never null.

**Valid values:**
- `"Homogeneous"` - All distractors from same category
- `"Heterogeneous"` - Mixed distractor types

**"Homogeneous"** = All distractors are the SAME TYPE:
- All drug names: "Abiraterone" / "Enzalutamide" / "Docetaxel" / "Olaparib" → **Homogeneous**
- All clinical actions: "Hold therapy" / "Dose reduce" / "Discontinue" → **Homogeneous**
- All trial names: "LATITUDE" / "VISION" / "PROfound" → **Homogeneous**

**"Heterogeneous"** = Distractors are DIFFERENT TYPES:
- Treatment + Safety + Mechanism: "Abiraterone" / "Anemia occurs in 50%" / "PARP targeting" → **Heterogeneous**

**Example - Question #536 (Homogeneous distractors):**
"Patient with mCRPC, PSMA-negative on PET. Next step?"
All answer options are treatment/management recommendations:
- "Initiate Lu-177-PSMA therapy"
- "Proceed with FDG PET imaging"
- "Continue current ADT and monitor"
- "Switch to alternative ARSI"
→ distractor_homogeneity: **"Homogeneous"** (all treatment/management choices)
→ answer_length_pattern: **"Uniform"** (all similar conceptual complexity)

**Rationale:** When all options represent the SAME TYPE of response (all treatments, all diagnostic tests, all monitoring strategies), tag as Homogeneous regardless of the specific drugs/tests mentioned. Here all options are clinical decision points about what to do next - they're conceptually the same type even though one is imaging and the others are therapeutic decisions.

### 68-73. flaw_absolute_terms, flaw_grammatical_cue, flaw_implausible_distractor, flaw_clang_association, flaw_convergence_vulnerability, flaw_double_negative
**REQUIRED** - These are boolean fields (true/false). Never null.

**Item-writing flaws to detect:**

**flaw_absolute_terms** (`true` / `false`):
- Words like "always", "never", "all", "none", "only" that signal incorrect options

**flaw_grammatical_cue** (`true` / `false`):
- Grammatical mismatch between stem and incorrect options

**flaw_implausible_distractor** (`true` / `false`):
- Obviously wrong distractor that no competent clinician would choose

**flaw_clang_association** (`true` / `false`):
- Word in stem repeated verbatim in correct answer (cueing)

**flaw_convergence_vulnerability** (`true` / `false`):
- Multiple distractors can be eliminated by same reasoning

**flaw_double_negative** (`true` / `false`):
- Double negative phrasing (confusing)

**Default for most well-written questions:** All flaws should be `false`

## Summary: Key Takeaways

1. **Castration status is EVERYTHING** - this goes in disease_type (mCRPC, nmCRPC, mHSPC)
2. **Screening questions are NON-ONCOLOGY** - flag for reclassification
3. **Neuroendocrine: auto-reclassify if unanimous, flag if conflict**
4. **Count treatment lines WITHIN CRPC** - not overall treatment history
5. **PSMA: only use "PSMA-positive" or "PSMA-negative"** - no other variants
6. **HRR mutations = PARP inhibitor eligible** - includes BRCA1/2, ATM, PALB2, etc.
7. **High-volume mHSPC = triplet therapy** (ADT + ARSI + docetaxel)
8. **BCR is distinct stage** - rising PSA after treatment with NO visible metastases
9. **Tag ALL fundable drugs** - including chemotherapy (docetaxel, cabazitaxel)
10. **PSA is usually NOT a biomarker** - only tag when PSA itself is the focus
