## CRITICAL: Non-Oncology Question Detection

**BEFORE tagging, verify this is truly an ONCOLOGY question.** Questions that MENTION CLL may actually be about a different specialty.

### Secondary Immunodeficiency (SID) Questions — Flag as NON-ONCOLOGY

Patients with CLL often develop secondary immunodeficiency. Questions about **managing infections or diagnosing immunodeficiency** belong to **Infectious Disease/Immunology**, NOT oncology.

**Red flags that suggest NON-ONCOLOGY (SID):**
1. Activity name contains "SID", "Secondary Immunodeficiency", or "Immunodeficiency"
2. Patient has CLL BUT the question asks about:
   - Infection diagnosis/management (CMV, PJP, fungal, pneumococcal, etc.)
   - Immunoglobulin replacement therapy (IVIG)
   - Vaccination in immunocompromised patients
   - Hypogammaglobulinemia workup (not related to CLL treatment)
3. Answer choices focus on immunology/infectious disease concepts, NOT cancer treatment

**If you detect this pattern:**
- Tag `topic: "Non-oncology - SID"` to flag for manual review
- The question will be removed from the oncology dataset and reclassified

**True oncology questions in infection context:**
- "How should you modify BTKi during active infection?" → ONCOLOGY (about CLL treatment modification)
- "Which CLL treatment has highest infection risk?" → ONCOLOGY (about CLL drug safety)

## CRITICAL: Compound Answer Handling

When the correct answer references options by letter/number (e.g., "B or C", "A and C", "Options 1 and 3"):

1. **DO NOT assume option order matches the original question** - The options you receive may be in a different order than the actual test question.

2. **Deduce the correct answer from clinical reasoning** - Read the substance of each option and determine which ones make sense as the correct answer based on the clinical scenario.

3. **Tag based on the deduced correct content** - Once you identify what the compound answer actually refers to, tag those treatments/concepts.

**Example (CLL):**
- **Stem:** 71-year-old man with treatment-naive CLL, unmutated IGHV, del(13q), no TP53 abnormality. On DOAC for atrial fibrillation, prefers minimal cardiac toxicity. What is the most appropriate frontline treatment?
- **Correct answer:** "B or C"
- **Options presented to you:** Acalabrutinib plus venetoclax ± obinutuzumab | Continuous ibrutinib monotherapy | Venetoclax plus obinutuzumab

**How to deduce:** The patient wants to avoid cardiac toxicity and has AFib. Ibrutinib has higher AFib risk than other BTKis. Therefore "B or C" most likely refers to:
- Acalabrutinib-based regimen (2nd-gen BTKi, lower cardiac risk)
- Venetoclax-obinutuzumab (no BTKi cardiac risk)

**Tag accordingly:** treatment_1: Acalabrutinib, treatment_2: Venetoclax, treatment_3: Obinutuzumab

## BTK INHIBITOR (BTKi) CLASSIFICATION RULES (CRITICAL)

BTK inhibitors are a cornerstone of CLL therapy. **Classify by binding mechanism:**

### Covalent vs Non-Covalent BTKi

| Mechanism | Binding | Key Feature | Examples |
|-----------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Covalent BTKi** | Irreversible (binds Cys481) | Susceptible to C481S resistance | ibrutinib, acalabrutinib, zanubrutinib |
| **Non-covalent BTKi** | Reversible | Active against C481S mutants | pirtobrutinib |

**For any BTK inhibitor encountered, determine if it is covalent or non-covalent. Look up unfamiliar agents to classify correctly.**

**Known covalent BTKi:**
- `"Ibrutinib"` - First-generation, broadest experience
- `"Acalabrutinib"` - Second-generation, more selective
- `"Zanubrutinib"` - Second-generation, more selective

**Known non-covalent BTKi:**
- `"Pirtobrutinib"` - Designed for C481S resistance, approved for R/R CLL after covalent BTKi

**Context-based inference:**
- "After ibrutinib resistance" or "C481S mutation" → likely pirtobrutinib (non-covalent)
- "Frontline BTKi" → covalent BTKi (ibrutinib, acalabrutinib, or zanubrutinib)
- "BTKi for del(17p)" → typically covalent BTKi frontline, non-covalent if post-BTKi

---

### BCL-2 Inhibitor

- `"Venetoclax"` - BCL-2 inhibitor, used alone or with anti-CD20

**Venetoclax-specific considerations:**
- Often combined with obinutuzumab (CLL14) or rituximab (MURANO)
- Fixed-duration regimens are a key differentiator from continuous BTKi
- TLS risk requires prophylaxis and ramp-up dosing

### Anti-CD20 Antibodies

- `"Obinutuzumab"` - Type II anti-CD20, glycoengineered (preferred with venetoclax)
- `"Rituximab"` - Type I anti-CD20 (used in FCR, VenR)
- `"Ofatumumab"` - Fully human anti-CD20 (less commonly used now)

### Chemoimmunotherapy (Tag only if specifically asked about)

- `"FCR"` - Fludarabine, cyclophosphamide, rituximab (fit, mutated IGHV)
- `"BR"` - Bendamustine + rituximab (less fit patients)

**Note:** Individual chemotherapy agents (fludarabine, bendamustine, cyclophosphamide) are NOT fundable targets unless specifically asked about.

### PI3K Inhibitors (Less commonly used)

- `"Idelalisib"` - PI3Kδ inhibitor (toxicity concerns limit use)
- `"Duvelisib"` - PI3Kδ/γ inhibitor (limited use)

### answer_length_pattern Criteria

**CRITICAL RULE:** If `distractor_homogeneity` = "Homogeneous", then `answer_length_pattern` is almost always "Uniform".

Tag "Variable" with Homogeneous distractors ONLY when one answer contains **additional clinical/educational content** the others lack - NOT when length differs due to:
- Grammatical phrasing ("The drug ibrutinib" vs "Ibrutinib")
- Compound answers ("Both A and B", "A and C", "All of the above")
- Minor word count differences

**Example - UNIFORM (despite length difference):**
- "Acalabrutinib/obinutuzumab only"
- "Acalabrutinib monotherapy only"
- "Both acalabrutinib monotherapy and acalabrutinib/obinutuzumab"
→ Third answer is longer but just combines options, no new clinical detail = **Uniform**

**"Uniform"** = All options are CONCEPTUALLY similar in complexity:
- All single drug names: "Ibrutinib" / "Acalabrutinib" / "Venetoclax" → **Uniform** (even if character counts differ)
- All short clinical phrases: "Continue therapy" / "Switch BTKi" / "Add venetoclax" → **Uniform**

**"Variable"** = Options differ in TYPE or COMPLEXITY AND contain different amounts of clinical detail:
- Mix of single words and detailed explanations: "Ibrutinib" / "Continue current venetoclax and monitor response" → **Variable**
- One answer has extra clinical context others lack: "BTKi" / "Add venetoclax after achieving remission with monitoring" → **Variable**

**"Correct longest/shortest"** = The CORRECT answer is noticeably longer/shorter than ALL distractors:
- Correct answer is a detailed explanation while distractors are single words → **Correct longest**
- Correct answer is a single drug while distractors are multi-drug regimens → **Correct shortest**

### distractor_homogeneity Criteria

**"Homogeneous"** = All distractors are the SAME TYPE of answer:
- All drug names: "Ibrutinib" / "Acalabrutinib" / "Venetoclax" / "Zanubrutinib" → **Homogeneous**
- All clinical actions: "Hold therapy" / "Dose reduce" / "Discontinue" / "Continue" → **Homogeneous**
- All trial names: "RESONATE" / "ALPINE" / "CLL14" / "MURANO" → **Homogeneous**
- Mix of single drugs and combos: "Zanubrutinib" / "Bendamustine + rituximab" / "Bortezomib" / "Venetoclax" → **Homogeneous** (all are treatment options, even if one is a combo or one is implausible for CLL)

**CRITICAL:** Implausible distractors (like bortezomib in CLL) are still the same TYPE (drug/treatment). Tag `flaw_implausible_distractor: true` but keep `distractor_homogeneity: "Homogeneous"`.

**"Heterogeneous"** = Distractors are DIFFERENT TYPES of answers:
- Treatment + Safety + Mechanism: "Ibrutinib" / "Atrial fibrillation occurs in 10%" / "BTK inhibition" → **Heterogeneous**
- Drug + Trial + Endpoint: "Venetoclax" / "CLL14 study" / "MRD negativity" → **Heterogeneous**
- Mix of domains: "Switch to zanubrutinib" / "Check for C481S" / "Refer for transplant" → **Heterogeneous**
