## ⚠️ CRITICAL: Non-Oncology Question Detection

**BEFORE tagging, verify this is truly an ONCOLOGY question.** Questions that MENTION GVHD may actually be about a different specialty.

### Secondary Immunodeficiency (SID) Questions — Flag as NON-ONCOLOGY

Patients with hematologic malignancies and cancer often develop secondary immunodeficiency. Questions about **managing infections or diagnosing immunodeficiency** belong to **Infectious Disease/Immunology**, NOT oncology.

**Red flags that suggest NON-ONCOLOGY (SID):**
1. Activity name contains "SID", "Secondary Immunodeficiency", or "Immunodeficiency"
2. Patient has GVHD BUT the question asks about:
   - Infection diagnosis/management (CMV, PJP, fungal, etc.)
   - Immunoglobulin replacement therapy
   - Vaccination in immunocompromised patients
   - Tests to diagnose immunodeficiency (not cancer)
3. Answer choices focus on immunology/infectious disease concepts, NOT cancer treatment

**If you detect this pattern:**
- Tag `topic: "Non-oncology - SID"` to flag for manual review
- The question will be removed from the oncology dataset and reclassified

**True oncology questions in infection context:**
- "How should you modify treatment during active infection?" → ONCOLOGY (about GVHD treatment modification)
- "Which GVHD treatment has highest infection risk?" → ONCOLOGY (about drug safety)

## CRITICAL: GVHD Context Determination

**GVHD has THREE distinct clinical contexts that MUST be distinguished:**

1. **GVHD Prophylaxis** - Prevention given at time of/after transplant to PREVENT GVHD
2. **GVHD Treatment** - Treatment given AFTER GVHD develops
3. **Acute vs Chronic GVHD** - Different disease types with different treatments

**How to distinguish:**
- "prophylaxis", "prevention", "to prevent GVHD" -> **Prophylaxis context**
- "patient with GVHD", "developed GVHD", "steroid-refractory", "after steroids" -> **Treatment context**
- "day 28 post-transplant", "within 100 days" -> **Acute GVHD**
- "7 months after transplant", "fasciitis", "sclerosis", "chronic" -> **Chronic GVHD**

---

## CRITICAL: Compound Answer Handling

When the correct answer references options by letter/number (e.g., "B or C", "A and C", "Options 1 and 3"):

1. **DO NOT assume option order matches the original question** - The options you receive may be in a different order than the actual test question.

2. **Deduce the correct answer from clinical reasoning** - Read the substance of each option and determine which ones make sense as the correct answer based on the clinical scenario.

3. **Tag based on the deduced correct content** - Once you identify what the compound answer actually refers to, tag those treatments/concepts.

**Example (GVHD):**
- **Stem:** 55-year-old with steroid-refractory chronic GVHD after prior ibrutinib. Which is the most appropriate next therapy?
- **Correct answer:** "A or B"
- **Options presented:** Belumosudil | Axatilimab | Rituximab | Best supportive care

**How to deduce:** Patient has steroid-refractory chronic GVHD, failed ibrutinib. Both belumosudil and axatilimab are approved for chronic GVHD after prior therapy.

## treatment_1-5: Critical Tagging Rules

**Rule 1: Subject vs Comparator**
Tag ONLY the drug that is the SUBJECT of the teaching point, not comparators.

EXAMPLE:
Q: "How does belumosudil compare to ruxolitinib for fibrotic chronic GVHD?"
A: "Belumosudil showed improvement in skin tightness"
- treatment_1: Belumosudil (subject being differentiated)
- DO NOT TAG: Ruxolitinib (comparator)

**Rule 2: Treatment as Clinical Context**
Tag treatments when they provide CONTEXT for interpreting a clinical finding.

EXAMPLE:
Q: "Patient on ruxolitinib develops cytopenias. What is the appropriate management?"
- treatment_1: Ruxolitinib
WHY: The cytopenia management depends on knowing the treatment.

**Rule 3: Drug Class -> Specific Approved Drugs**
When the correct answer is a drug CLASS, you MUST tag all approved specific drugs.

| Answer Class | Tag ALL of these |
|--------------|------------------|
| "JAK inhibitor" (for GVHD) | Ruxolitinib |
| "ROCK2 inhibitor" | Belumosudil |
| "BTK inhibitor" (for GVHD) | Ibrutinib |
| "CSF-1R inhibitor" | Axatilimab |

**Rule 4: Drug Class Expansion for AE Management and Safety Profile**
When topic is "AE management" or "Safety profile" and the question mentions a drug CLASS (not a specific drug), infer and tag all approved specific drugs for that class in this disease using the drug class table above.
- Use disease context and STARTDATE to determine which specific drugs were approved
- Example: "Patient on a CAR-T therapy develops CRS" → tag all approved CAR-T products for this disease

Override to "Variable" or "Correct longest/shortest" ONLY when the length difference is EGREGIOUS — e.g., one answer is a single word and another is a full sentence with additional clinical detail.

- Drug name lengths differ

**When in doubt with Homogeneous distractors → tag "Uniform".**

- Grammatical phrasing

- Minor word count differences

**"Uniform"** = All options are CONCEPTUALLY similar in complexity:

**"Variable"** = Options differ in TYPE or COMPLEXITY AND contain different amounts of clinical detail

## Important Guidelines for GVHD

1. **Topic = Question type, NOT clinical setting** - "Which prophylaxis?" = Treatment selection
2. **Prophylaxis vs Treatment in treatment_line** - "Prophylaxis" vs "1L", "2L", "3L", "4L+"
3. **Acute vs Chronic in disease_type_1** - Different disease biology, different approved agents
4. **Steroid-naive vs Steroid-refractory in disease_type_2** - Disease status (use consistently)
5. **Treatment line logic** - 1L (steroids), 2L (first targeted after steroids), 3L (after steroids + 1 targeted), 4L+ (after steroids + 2+ targeted)
6. **Tag ALL treatments for GVHD** - Including PTCy, tacrolimus, MMF, steroids (backbone agents)
7. **Axatilimab/Belumosudil default** - Assume steroid-refractory unless stated otherwise
8. **Organ involvement in symptom fields** - Fasciitis, diarrhea, elevated LFTs, etc.
9. **NIH severity in disease_specific_factor** - NIH mild, moderate, severe
10. **Tag from CORRECT answer only** - Don't tag distractors
   - **Standard questions ("Which of the following...")**: Tag ONLY entities from the correct answer
   - **Negative lead-ins ("All EXCEPT", "Which is NOT")**: Tag the INCORRECT options (distractors) instead, as they represent the true/factual statements. The correct answer is the exception and should NOT be tagged as a treatment/biomarker.
   - **Compound answers ("A and B", "All of the above")**: Tag all components that make up the correct answer
