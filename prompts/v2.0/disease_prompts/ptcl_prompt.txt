## ⚠️ CRITICAL: Non-Oncology Question Detection

**BEFORE tagging, verify this is truly an ONCOLOGY question.** Questions that MENTION PTCL may actually be about a different specialty.

### Secondary Immunodeficiency (SID) Questions — Flag as NON-ONCOLOGY

Patients with hematologic malignancies and cancer often develop secondary immunodeficiency. Questions about **managing infections or diagnosing immunodeficiency** belong to **Infectious Disease/Immunology**, NOT oncology.

**Red flags that suggest NON-ONCOLOGY (SID):**
1. Activity name contains "SID", "Secondary Immunodeficiency", or "Immunodeficiency"
2. Patient has PTCL BUT the question asks about:
   - Infection diagnosis/management (CMV, PJP, fungal, etc.)
   - Immunoglobulin replacement therapy
   - Vaccination in immunocompromised patients
   - Tests to diagnose immunodeficiency (not cancer)
3. Answer choices focus on immunology/infectious disease concepts, NOT cancer treatment

**If you detect this pattern:**
- Tag `topic: "Non-oncology - SID"` to flag for manual review
- The question will be removed from the oncology dataset and reclassified

**True oncology questions in infection context:**
- "How should you modify treatment during active infection?" → ONCOLOGY (about PTCL treatment modification)
- "Which PTCL treatment has highest infection risk?" → ONCOLOGY (about drug safety)

## PTCL Overview

**PTCL subtypes:**
- **PTCL-NOS** (not otherwise specified) - most common, heterogeneous
- **AITL** (Angioimmunoblastic T-cell lymphoma) - EBV-associated, autoimmune features
- **ALCL, ALK-positive** - Better prognosis, younger patients
- **ALCL, ALK-negative** - Worse prognosis than ALK+
- **ATLL** (Adult T-cell leukemia/lymphoma) - HTLV-1 associated
- **Extranodal NK/T-cell lymphoma, nasal type** - EBV-associated, Asian prevalence
- **Hepatosplenic T-cell lymphoma** - Young males, poor prognosis

**Key difference from B-cell lymphomas:** No rituximab (T-cell origin)

### ⚠️ Topic Disambiguation

**"Indication" vs "Efficacy":**
- "Which patients qualify for brentuximab vedotin?" → **Treatment indication**
- "What were the results of ECHELON-2?" → **Clinical efficacy**

**"Safety profile" vs "Prophylaxis" vs "AE management":**
- **Safety profile** = knowing WHAT AEs to expect (awareness)
- **Prophylaxis** = how to PREVENT toxicities BEFORE they occur (folate/B12 with pralatrexate)
- **AE management** = what to DO after an AE has occurred (treating mucositis)

### 7-11. treatment_1 through treatment_5

**CRITICAL TAGGING RULES:**

**Rule 1: Tag from CORRECT ANSWER only**
Do NOT tag drugs from distractors.
- **Standard questions ("Which of the following...")**: Tag ONLY entities from the correct answer
- **Negative lead-ins ("All EXCEPT", "Which is NOT")**: Tag the INCORRECT options (distractors) instead, as they represent the true/factual statements. The correct answer is the exception and should NOT be tagged as a treatment/biomarker.
- **Compound answers ("A and B", "All of the above")**: Tag all components that make up the correct answer

**Rule 2: Subject vs Comparator**
Tag ONLY the drug that is the SUBJECT of the teaching point.

EXAMPLE:
Q: "Which agent showed superior PFS compared to CHOP in CD30+ PTCL?"
A: "Brentuximab vedotin + CHP"
✓ treatment_1: Brentuximab vedotin (subject - showed superiority)
✗ DO NOT TAG: CHOP (comparator)

**Rule 3: Drug Class → Specific Drugs**
When answer is a drug CLASS, tag all approved specific drugs.

| Answer Class | Tag ALL of these |
|--------------|------------------|
| "HDAC inhibitor" | Romidepsin, Belinostat |
| "Antibody-drug conjugate (ADC)" | Brentuximab vedotin |
| "PI3K inhibitor" | Duvelisib |

- Brentuximab vedotin, Romidepsin, Belinostat, Pralatrexate

- CHOP (only if specifically taught, not just mentioned as backbone)

**Rule 4: Drug Class Expansion for AE Management and Safety Profile**
When topic is "AE management" or "Safety profile" and the question mentions a drug CLASS (not a specific drug), infer and tag all approved specific drugs for that class in this disease using the drug class table above.
- Use disease context and STARTDATE to determine which specific drugs were approved
- Example: "Patient on a CAR-T therapy develops CRS" → tag all approved CAR-T products for this disease
- Example: "BTKi-associated atrial fibrillation management" → tag all approved BTKi drugs for this disease
- The causative drug class is clinically relevant — funders need to know WHICH specific drugs are associated with the AE
**Rule 5: Do NOT Tag Supportive Care Drugs as Treatments**
Tocilizumab, corticosteroids (dexamethasone for CRS), and other supportive care agents used to MANAGE adverse events should NOT be tagged in treatment_1-5 fields. These are AE management interventions, not the disease-directed therapies of interest to funders.
- Example: "Patient develops Grade 2 CRS, treated with tocilizumab" -> Tag the causative therapy (CAR-T/bispecific) in treatment_1. Do NOT tag tocilizumab.
- Exception: If the question is SPECIFICALLY about tocilizumab pharmacology or clinical data, then it may be tagged.
- This applies to ALL CRS/ICANS management agents across CAR-T and bispecific antibody questions.

**DEFAULT: If `distractor_homogeneity` = "Homogeneous", ALWAYS tag `answer_length_pattern` = "Uniform".**

Do NOT tag "Variable" just because:
- One option has a few extra words
- Compound answers are longer ("Both A and B" vs "A only")
- Drug name lengths differ

**When in doubt with Homogeneous distractors → tag "Uniform".**
