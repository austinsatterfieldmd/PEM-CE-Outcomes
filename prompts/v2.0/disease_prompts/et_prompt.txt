## ET DRUG LANDSCAPE (CRITICAL)

### Platelet-Lowering Agent
| Drug | Status | Context | Notes |
|------|--------|---------|-------|
| **Anagrelide** | FDA approved | 1L or 2L for ET | Selective platelet-lowering, inhibits megakaryocyte maturation |

**Key Points:**
- Reduces platelet count without affecting other cell lines
- Can be used as monotherapy or with hydroxyurea
- Cardiac effects (palpitations, CHF) - use cautiously in cardiac disease

### Interferons
| Drug | Status | Context | Notes |
|------|--------|---------|-------|
| **Ropeginterferon alfa-2b** (Besremi) | FDA approved | For PV, off-label for ET | May achieve molecular responses |
| **Peginterferon alfa-2a** | Off-label | 1L or 2L | Preferred in young patients, pregnancy |

**Key Points:**
- May reduce JAK2/CALR allele burden
- Preferred in young patients wanting to preserve fertility
- Can delay/prevent disease transformation

### JAK Inhibitor (Limited Role in ET)
| Drug | Status | Context |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Ruxolitinib** | Off-label in ET | May be used in symptomatic splenomegaly |

**NOTE:** Unlike PV, ruxolitinib is NOT FDA-approved for ET

### Backbone Cytoreduction (DO NOT TAG unless specifically asked about)
- Hydroxyurea (HU) - First-line cytoreduction (standard of care)
- Aspirin - Standard antiplatelet
- **Exception:** Tag if question specifically asks about these agents

### 7. biomarker_1 through biomarker_5 (up to 5 biomarkers)
**CRITICAL for ET** - Driver mutations affect prognosis and treatment.

| Biomarker | Frequency | Clinical Significance |
|-----------|-----------|----------------------|

- JAK2 V617F+ + Age >=60 = High-risk (IPSET-thrombosis)
- CALR+ generally has better prognosis than JAK2+
- Triple-negative has poorer prognosis

## Important Guidelines for ET

1. **IPSET-thrombosis risk stratification** - Very low, Low, Intermediate, High
2. **JAK2 V617F increases thrombosis risk** - Part of risk stratification
3. **CALR mutation has better prognosis** - Lower thrombosis risk than JAK2
4. **Prior thrombosis = high-risk** - Regardless of age or mutation
5. **Anagrelide has cardiac effects** - Caution in cardiac disease
6. **Interferon preferred in young/pregnant** - Can achieve molecular responses
7. **Extreme thrombocytosis can cause bleeding** - Acquired von Willebrand syndrome
8. **Tag from CORRECT answer only** - Don't tag distractors
