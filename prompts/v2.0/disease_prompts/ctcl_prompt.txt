## ⚠️ CRITICAL: Non-Oncology Question Detection

**BEFORE tagging, verify this is truly an ONCOLOGY question.** Questions that MENTION CTCL may actually be about a different specialty.

### Secondary Immunodeficiency (SID) Questions — Flag as NON-ONCOLOGY

Patients with hematologic malignancies and cancer often develop secondary immunodeficiency. Questions about **managing infections or diagnosing immunodeficiency** belong to **Infectious Disease/Immunology**, NOT oncology.

**Red flags that suggest NON-ONCOLOGY (SID):**
1. Activity name contains "SID", "Secondary Immunodeficiency", or "Immunodeficiency"
2. Patient has CTCL BUT the question asks about:
   - Infection diagnosis/management (CMV, PJP, fungal, etc.)
   - Immunoglobulin replacement therapy
   - Vaccination in immunocompromised patients
   - Tests to diagnose immunodeficiency (not cancer)
3. Answer choices focus on immunology/infectious disease concepts, NOT cancer treatment

**If you detect this pattern:**
- Tag `topic: "Non-oncology - SID"` to flag for manual review
- The question will be removed from the oncology dataset and reclassified

**True oncology questions in infection context:**
- "How should you modify treatment during active infection?" → ONCOLOGY (about CTCL treatment modification)
- "Which CTCL treatment has highest infection risk?" → ONCOLOGY (about drug safety)

## CTCL Overview

**CTCL subtypes:**
- **Mycosis fungoides (MF)** - Most common, patch/plaque/tumor stages
- **Sézary syndrome (SS)** - Leukemic variant, erythroderma, poor prognosis

**Staging:**
- Early-stage (IA-IIA): Limited skin involvement
- Advanced-stage (IIB-IVB): Tumors, nodal, visceral, or blood involvement

## Group A: Core Fields (20 fields)

### 1. topic
**REQUIRED** - Every question must have exactly one topic. Never null.

| Topic | Use When... |
|-------|-------------|
| **Treatment selection** | Choosing WHICH drug/regimen - skin-directed vs systemic |
| **Treatment indication** | Whether a patient MEETS CRITERIA for treatment escalation |
| **Clinical efficacy** | Response rates, survival outcomes, trial results |
| **Safety profile** | Knowing WHAT AEs to expect |
| **AE management** | What to DO about an AE already occurring |
| **Prophylaxis** | How to PREVENT toxicities BEFORE they occur |
| **Biomarker testing** | CD30 testing, Sézary cell quantification |
| **Mechanism of action** | Drug targets, pathways |
| **Diagnosis** | MF vs SS differentiation, staging |
| **Prognosis** | Risk factors, staging implications |

### ⚠️ Topic Disambiguation

**"Indication" vs "Efficacy":**
- "Who is eligible for mogamulizumab?" → **Treatment indication**
- "What were the results of MAVORIC?" → **Clinical efficacy**

**"Safety profile" vs "Prophylaxis" vs "AE management":**
- **Safety profile** = knowing WHAT AEs to expect (mogamulizumab skin rash)
- **Prophylaxis** = how to PREVENT toxicities BEFORE they occur
- **AE management** = what to DO after an AE has occurred (hold, dose reduce)

✗ DO NOT TAG: Vorinostat (comparator)

## Group G: Question Format/Quality (15 fields)

### 56. cme_outcome_level
**Values:** `"3 - Knowledge"` or `"4 - Competence"`

**CRITICAL RULE:** If the clinical vignette is NOT REQUIRED to answer the question, it's **Level 3 (Knowledge)**, not Level 4.

**Test:** Could someone answer correctly WITHOUT reading the patient details?
- YES → `"3 - Knowledge"`
- NO → `"4 - Competence"`

### 57-60. Format fields
- `data_response_type`: "Numeric", "Qualitative", "Comparative", "Boolean"
- `stem_type`: "Clinical vignette", "Direct question", "Incomplete statement"
- `lead_in_type`: "Standard", "Negative (EXCEPT/NOT)", "Best answer"
- `answer_format`: "Single best", "Compound (A+B)", "All of the above", "None of the above"

### 61. answer_length_pattern
**Values:** "Uniform", "Variable", "Correct longest", "Correct shortest"

**DEFAULT: If `distractor_homogeneity` = "Homogeneous", ALWAYS tag `answer_length_pattern` = "Uniform".**

Override to "Variable" or "Correct longest/shortest" ONLY when the length difference is EGREGIOUS — e.g., one answer is a single word and another is a full sentence with additional clinical detail.

Do NOT tag "Variable" just because:
- One option has a few extra words
- Compound answers are longer ("Both A and B" vs "A only")
- Drug name lengths differ

**When in doubt with Homogeneous distractors → tag "Uniform".**

### 62. distractor_homogeneity
**"Homogeneous"** = All options are the SAME TYPE (all drugs, all actions)
**"Heterogeneous"** = Options are DIFFERENT TYPES

**Answer:** "Brentuximab vedotin"

**Rationale:**
- Patient vignette is REQUIRED (CD30 status + prior therapies) → Level 4
- CD30 ≥10% makes patient eligible for brentuximab vedotin
- Stage IIB = Advanced-stage
- Two prior therapies = R/R

### Example 2: Sézary Syndrome Diagnosis

**Question:** "Which finding is required to diagnose Sézary syndrome rather than erythrodermic mycosis fungoides?"

**Answer:** "Circulating Sézary cells ≥1000/µL"

```json

    ...
}
```
**Rationale:**
- Direct question about diagnostic criteria → Level 3 (recall)
- Topic is Diagnosis (subtype differentiation)
- Sézary cells in blood is the key differentiating biomarker

### Example 3: MAVORIC Trial Results

**Question:** "In the MAVORIC trial, mogamulizumab was compared to which agent in patients with relapsed/refractory MF or Sézary syndrome?"

**Answer:** "Vorinostat"

**Rationale:**
- Direct question about trial design → Level 3 (recall)
- Topic is Clinical efficacy (trial context)
- Tag mogamulizumab as the investigational drug of interest
