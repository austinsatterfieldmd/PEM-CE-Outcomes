## WM Overview

**Definition:** Lymphoplasmacytic lymphoma (LPL) with bone marrow involvement and IgM monoclonal gammopathy

**Key features:**
- **MYD88 L265P mutation** (>90% of cases) - diagnostic and predictive
- **CXCR4 mutations** (30-40%, affect treatment response to BTK inhibitors)
- **Hyperviscosity syndrome** from elevated IgM
- **No staging system** - treatment based on symptoms, not stage

**Treatment indication:** Symptomatic WM (cytopenias, hyperviscosity, neuropathy, bulky disease)

### BTK Inhibitors (Mainstay of Treatment)
| Drug | Notes | Key Trial |
|------|-------|-----------|
| **Ibrutinib** | First approved, MYD88 mutated respond best | iNNOVATE |
| **Zanubrutinib** | Better tolerated, fewer cardiac AEs | ASPEN |
| **Acalabrutinib** | Used but less WM-specific data | - |

### Other Agents
| Drug | Class | Context |
|------|-------|---------|
| **Rituximab** | Monoclonal antibody | Often combined, watch for IgM flare |
| **Bendamustine** | Alkylator | With rituximab (BR) |
| **Bortezomib** | Proteasome inhibitor | BDR regimen |
| **Venetoclax** | BCL-2 inhibitor | Emerging option |

### Key Considerations
- **IgM flare** with rituximab - avoid if IgM >4000 or symptoms
- **CXCR4 mutations** - slower response to BTK inhibitors
- **MYD88 wild-type** - consider non-WM diagnosis, poor BTK response
