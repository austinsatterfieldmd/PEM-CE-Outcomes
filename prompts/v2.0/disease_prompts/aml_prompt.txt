## ⚠️ CRITICAL: Non-Oncology Question Detection

**BEFORE tagging, verify this is truly an ONCOLOGY question.** Questions that MENTION AML may actually be about a different specialty.

### Secondary Immunodeficiency (SID) Questions — Flag as NON-ONCOLOGY

Patients with hematologic malignancies and cancer often develop secondary immunodeficiency. Questions about **managing infections or diagnosing immunodeficiency** belong to **Infectious Disease/Immunology**, NOT oncology.

**Red flags that suggest NON-ONCOLOGY (SID):**
1. Activity name contains "SID", "Secondary Immunodeficiency", or "Immunodeficiency"
2. Patient has AML BUT the question asks about:
   - Infection diagnosis/management (CMV, PJP, fungal, etc.)
   - Immunoglobulin replacement therapy
   - Vaccination in immunocompromised patients
   - Tests to diagnose immunodeficiency (not cancer)
3. Answer choices focus on immunology/infectious disease concepts, NOT cancer treatment

**If you detect this pattern:**
- Tag `topic: "Non-oncology - SID"` to flag for manual review
- The question will be removed from the oncology dataset and reclassified

**True oncology questions in infection context:**
- "How should you modify treatment during active infection?" → ONCOLOGY (about AML treatment modification)
- "Which AML treatment has highest infection risk?" → ONCOLOGY (about drug safety)

## CRITICAL: Compound Answer Handling

When the correct answer references options by letter/number (e.g., "B or C", "A and C", "Options 1 and 3"):

1. **DO NOT assume option order matches the original question** - The options you receive may be in a different order than the actual test question.

2. **Deduce the correct answer from clinical reasoning** - Read the substance of each option and determine which ones make sense as the correct answer based on the clinical scenario.

3. **Tag based on the deduced correct content** - Once you identify what the compound answer actually refers to, tag those treatments/concepts.

**Example (AML):**
- **Stem:** 68-year-old with newly diagnosed AML, IDH1 mutation, unfit for intensive chemo. What is the most appropriate frontline treatment?
- **Correct answer:** "A or B"
- **Options presented:** Ivosidenib + azacitidine | Venetoclax + azacitidine | 7+3 induction | Best supportive care

**How to deduce:** The patient is unfit for intensive chemo and has IDH1 mutation. Therefore "A or B" most likely refers to:
- Ivosidenib + azacitidine (IDH1-targeted, non-intensive)
- Venetoclax + azacitidine (non-intensive standard)

**Tag accordingly:** treatment_1: Ivosidenib, treatment_2: Venetoclax, treatment_3: Azacitidine

## Group A: Core Fields (20 fields)

### 1. topic
**REQUIRED** - Every question must have exactly one topic. Never null.

| Topic | Use When... |
|-------|-------------|
| **Treatment selection** | Choosing WHICH drug/regimen to use for a patient (patient vignette + "which therapy") |
| **Treatment indication** | Whether a patient MEETS CRITERIA for a treatment - eligibility, candidacy, **approved indications**. "Which patients are eligible for venetoclax-based therapy?", "Who qualifies for intensive chemotherapy?" |
| **Clinical efficacy** | Response rates, survival outcomes, clinical trial results, "key findings", **"what were the results of VIALE-A trial"** |

**Topic Disambiguation - "Indication" vs "Efficacy":**
- "Where is ivosidenib **indicated**?" -> **Treatment indication** (asking about approved label/indications)
- "Where has ivosidenib **demonstrated efficacy**?" -> **Clinical efficacy** (asking about trial results/outcomes)

**Topic Disambiguation - Safety profile vs Prophylaxis vs AE management:**
- **Safety profile** = knowing WHAT adverse events to expect (awareness, education, recognition)
- **Prophylaxis** = knowing HOW TO PREVENT toxicities BEFORE they occur (prevention, premedication)
- **AE management** = knowing WHAT TO DO after an AE has already occurred (intervention, dose modification)

**AML-specific topic examples:**
- "What is differentiation syndrome with IDH inhibitors?" -> **Safety profile**
- "What premedication is needed before venetoclax?" -> **Prophylaxis**
- "Patient develops QTc prolongation on gilteritinib. What do you do?" -> **AE management**
- "What is the mechanism of venetoclax?" -> **Mechanism of action**
- "What does FLT3-ITD allelic ratio mean for prognosis?" -> **Prognosis** or **Biomarker testing**

| **Safety profile** | Knowing WHAT AEs to expect or recognize - differentiation syndrome, QTc prolongation, tumor lysis syndrome |
| **AE management** | What to DO about an AE already occurring - hold drug, dose-reduce, manage differentiation syndrome |
| **Prophylaxis** | Knowing HOW TO PREVENT toxicities BEFORE they occur - TLS prophylaxis, infection prevention |
| **Biomarker testing** | Mutation testing, cytogenetic testing, molecular profiling, FLT3/IDH/NPM1 testing |

| **Shared decision-making** | Patient-provider communication, discussing treatment options |
| **Disparities in care** | Racial/ethnic disparities, access issues in AML |
| **Barriers to care** | Obstacles to treatment (cost, access, adherence) |

### 2. disease_stage
**ALWAYS NULL for AML** - AML does not have a conventional staging system.

Unlike solid tumors (TNM), AML is not staged. Disease classification uses:
- ELN 2022 risk stratification -> `disease_type_1`
- Molecular features -> `biomarker`
- De novo vs secondary vs therapy-related etiology -> `disease_type_2`

**Always use `null` for this field in AML.**

### 3-4. disease_type_1, disease_type_2
**OPTIONAL for AML**

**disease_type_1 = ELN 2022 Risk Category (when explicitly stated):**
- `"Favorable"` - t(8;21), inv(16), NPM1 without FLT3-ITD high, bZIP CEBPA
- `"Intermediate"` - NPM1 + FLT3-ITD, wild-type NPM1 without adverse features
- `"Adverse"` - TP53, RUNX1, ASXL1, complex karyotype, monosomal, del(5q), -7, KMT2Ar

**disease_type_2 = AML etiology/subtype origin (when explicitly stated):**
- `"De novo AML"` - Primary AML without prior hematologic disorder
- `"Secondary AML"` - AML arising from prior MDS, MPN, or other hematologic disorder
- `"Therapy-related AML"` (t-AML) - AML after prior chemotherapy/radiation

**Note:** Specific genetic abnormalities (FLT3-ITD, NPM1, IDH1, IDH2, KMT2Ar) go in the **biomarker** fields, not disease_type.

**Common patterns:**
- "Favorable-risk AML" -> disease_type_1: "Favorable"
- "Adverse-risk AML" -> disease_type_1: "Adverse"
- "Secondary AML after prior MDS" -> disease_type_2: "Secondary AML"
- "Therapy-related AML after prior breast cancer chemotherapy" -> disease_type_2: "Therapy-related AML"
- Generic "AML" without risk or etiology stated -> disease_type_1: null, disease_type_2: null

## treatment_1-5: Critical Tagging Rules

**Rule 1: Subject vs Comparator**
Tag ONLY the drug that is the SUBJECT of the teaching point, not comparators.

EXAMPLE:
Q: "Which AE differentiates venetoclax from intensive chemotherapy?"
A: "Tumor lysis syndrome"
- treatment_1: Venetoclax (subject being differentiated)
- DO NOT TAG: intensive chemotherapy (comparator)

**Rule 2: Treatment as Clinical Context**
Tag treatments when they provide CONTEXT for interpreting a clinical finding.

EXAMPLE:
Q: "Patient on gilteritinib develops QTc prolongation. What is the most likely cause?"
- treatment_1: Gilteritinib
WHY: The QTc interpretation depends on knowing the treatment regimen.

**Rule 3: Drug Class -> Specific Approved Drugs**
When the correct answer is a drug CLASS, you MUST tag all approved specific drugs.

| Answer Class | Tag ALL of these |
|--------------|------------------|
| "FLT3 inhibitor" (newly diagnosed) | Midostaurin, Quizartinib |
| "FLT3 inhibitor" (R/R) | Gilteritinib, Quizartinib |
| "Menin inhibitor" | Revumenib, Ziftomenib |
| "IDH1 inhibitor" | Ivosidenib, Olutasidenib |
| "IDH2 inhibitor" | Enasidenib |
| "IDH inhibitor" (generic) | Clarify which mutation - IDH1 vs IDH2 |
| "BCL-2 inhibitor" | Venetoclax |
| "HMA" | Azacitidine, Decitabine, Decitabine/cedazuridine |

**Rule 4: Drug Class Expansion for AE Management and Safety Profile**
When topic is "AE management" or "Safety profile" and the question mentions a drug CLASS (not a specific drug), infer and tag all approved specific drugs for that class in this disease using the drug class table above.
- Use disease context and STARTDATE to determine which specific drugs were approved

### answer_length_pattern Criteria (CRITICAL - Common Error)

**DEFAULT: If `distractor_homogeneity` = "Homogeneous", ALWAYS tag `answer_length_pattern` = "Uniform".**

Override to "Variable" or "Correct longest/shortest" ONLY when the length difference is EGREGIOUS — e.g., one answer is a single word and another is a full sentence with additional clinical detail.

Do NOT tag "Variable" just because:
- One option has a few extra words
- Compound answers are longer ("Both A and B" vs "A only")
- Drug name lengths differ

**When in doubt with Homogeneous distractors → tag "Uniform".**

Tag "Variable" with Homogeneous distractors ONLY when one answer contains **additional clinical/educational content** the others lack - NOT when length differs due to:
- Grammatical phrasing ("The drug gilteritinib" vs "Gilteritinib")
- Compound answers ("Both A and B", "A and C", "All of the above")
- Minor word count differences

**Example - UNIFORM (despite length difference):**
- "Venetoclax only"
- "Azacitidine only"
- "Both venetoclax and azacitidine"
-> Third answer is longer but just combines options, no new clinical detail = **Uniform**

**"Uniform"** = All options are CONCEPTUALLY similar in complexity:
- All single drug names: "Gilteritinib" / "Midostaurin" / "Quizartinib" -> **Uniform** (even if character counts differ)
- All short clinical phrases: "Continue therapy" / "Switch treatment" / "Proceed to transplant" -> **Uniform**

**"Variable"** = Options differ in TYPE or COMPLEXITY AND contain different amounts of clinical detail:
- Mix of single words and detailed explanations: "Gilteritinib" / "Continue current chemotherapy and monitor for response with repeat bone marrow" -> **Variable**
- One answer has extra clinical context others lack: "FLT3 inhibitor" / "Add venetoclax after achieving remission with MRD monitoring" -> **Variable**

**"Correct longest/shortest"** = The CORRECT answer is noticeably longer/shorter than ALL distractors:
- Correct answer is a detailed explanation while distractors are single words -> **Correct longest**
- Correct answer is a single drug while distractors are multi-drug regimens -> **Correct shortest**

**IMPORTANT:** If answer lengths ARE variable AND the correct answer is the longest -> tag `"Correct longest"` (not just "Variable"). Similarly for shortest. These are more specific than "Variable" and should be used when applicable.

### distractor_homogeneity Criteria (CRITICAL - Common Error)

**"Homogeneous"** = All distractors are the SAME TYPE of answer:
- All drug names: "Gilteritinib" / "Midostaurin" / "Venetoclax" / "Ivosidenib" -> **Homogeneous**
- All clinical actions: "Hold therapy" / "Dose reduce" / "Discontinue" / "Continue" -> **Homogeneous**
- All trial names: "RATIFY" / "ADMIRAL" / "VIALE-A" / "AGILE" -> **Homogeneous**
- Mix of single drugs and combos: "Venetoclax" / "Ven/aza" / "7+3" / "CPX-351" -> **Homogeneous** (all are treatment options)

**CRITICAL:** Implausible distractors are still the same TYPE. Tag `flaw_implausible_distractor: true` but keep `distractor_homogeneity: "Homogeneous"` if all options are drugs/treatments.

**"Heterogeneous"** = Distractors are DIFFERENT TYPES of answers:
- Treatment + Safety + Mechanism: "Gilteritinib" / "Differentiation syndrome occurs in 20%" / "FLT3 inhibition" -> **Heterogeneous**
- Drug + Trial + Endpoint: "Midostaurin" / "RATIFY study" / "Overall survival" -> **Heterogeneous**
- Mix of domains: "Add FLT3 inhibitor" / "Monitor for TLS" / "Proceed to transplant" -> **Heterogeneous**
