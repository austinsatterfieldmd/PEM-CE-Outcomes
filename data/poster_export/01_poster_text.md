# Tracking Educational Impact in Relapsed/Refractory Myeloma Across Multidisciplinary Learners

**Authors:** Eli Anseaume, Steve Nair, MD, Joshua Barbach, MS, Ashley Romano
**Institution:** Physicians' Education Resource, LLC., Cranbury, NJ

---

## BACKGROUND

Multiple myeloma treatment has been transformed by novel immunotherapies including CAR-T cell therapy, bispecific antibodies, and antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs). As these therapies move from academic centers into community practice, ensuring all members of the multidisciplinary care team—hematologist-oncologists, advanced practice providers (APPs), and oncology nurses—have the knowledge to optimize patient outcomes is critical. Understanding baseline knowledge gaps and educational impact across different learner groups enables targeted curriculum development and resource allocation.

---

## OBJECTIVE

To evaluate longitudinal educational outcomes in relapsed/refractory (R/R) multiple myeloma across multidisciplinary learners, specifically:
1. Compare knowledge gains between academic and community hematologist-oncologists on prescribing-related topics
2. Assess whether APPs can close the knowledge gap with physicians through CME
3. Determine if nurses demonstrate greater improvement on role-relevant supportive care topics
4. Track year-over-year trends in baseline knowledge and educational impact

---

## METHODS

- **Data source**: Data from 40 accredited CME/CE initiatives across multiple modalities (live in-person, live virtual, or online on-demand) (2022-2025)
- **Population**: 10,357 learner responses across 95 unique questions focused on CAR-T, bispecific antibodies, and ADCs in R/R multiple myeloma
- **Learner groups**: Hematologist-oncologists (n=764), APPs (n=650), oncology nurses (n=5,846)
- **Topic classification**: Questions categorized as prescribing-focused (clinical efficacy, mechanism of action, treatment indication, treatment selection) or supportive care-focused (safety profile, AE management, shared decision-making, disparities in care)
- **Analysis**: Pre/post percentage scores calculated as (correct responses / total responses) × 100; statistical significance assessed via z-test for proportions (α=0.05)

---

## CONCLUSIONS AND FUTURE DIRECTIONS

**Key Findings:**
- **Consistent educational impact**: 17-20 percentage point improvement maintained across all 4 years regardless of learner type
- **APPs close the gap**: Started 8.6pp behind physicians (p<0.001), narrowed to 5.9pp after education—demonstrating CME effectively upskills the multidisciplinary team
- **Role-aligned learning**: Nurses showed 4.2pp greater improvement on supportive care topics vs. other topics, with shared decision-making (+24.4pp) and AE management (+21.6pp) showing largest gains
- **No academic-community divide**: Overall prescribing knowledge equivalent between practice settings, though CAR-T showed academic advantage reflecting hands-on experience

**Future Directions:**
- Develop role-specific educational pathways optimized for each discipline's scope of practice
- Expand longitudinal tracking to emerging therapies (GPRC5D-targeted bispecifics, next-generation CAR-T)
- Investigate whether educational gains translate to improved patient outcomes through practice change assessments
- Apply AI-powered question tagging to enable real-time identification of knowledge gaps across therapeutic areas
